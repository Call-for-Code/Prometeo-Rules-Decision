In the USA
 * OSHA sets the law on exposure hazards, but the OSHA regulations are old.
 * NIOSH is a research wing of the CDC that makes updated recommendations to OSHA, so it's limits tend to be more up to date (safer).
 * Similar to NIOSH, ACGIH also makes recommendations about safety alone, without reference practicality.
In Europe 
 * EU-OSHA has a similar regulatory role to OSHAc.
 * SCOEL has a similar advisory role to NIOSH.
